What is the geometry meaning of determinant

Determinant of matrix A is written as det(A).

One explanation of determinant is the directed area or directed volume of a superparallel polyhedron composed of rows or column vectors in the determinant. Usually, it is composed of column vectors.

Another explanation is that determinant tells the scaling of area or volume of linear transformation.
